Introduction

Programming is not just about writing commands; it is the evolution of the art of logical problem-solving that began centuries ago. This journey shows how complex mechanical machines transformed into powerful digital computers that form the basis of our modern world.

Ada Lovelace: The First Computer Programmer

Date: 1815 - 1852

Ada Lovelace worked with Charles Babbage on the Analytical Engine. She wrote a detailed algorithm intended to be processed by this machine, which is considered the first computer program in history.

3. Emergence of High-Level Languages

Date: 1950s and 1960s.

Examples: FORTRAN and COBOL.

Importance: Transitioning from complex machine languages to languages resembling English made programming faster and accessible to more people.

4. The Era of Personal Computers and the Internet

Date: 1980s to the new millennium.

Examples: The rise of graphical operating systems, and the development of languages such as C, C++, and Java.

Importance: Programming became consumer-oriented, and the Internet opened the door for web application development.
